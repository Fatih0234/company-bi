# SwiftRide NYC Market Entry Playbook

**Audience:** Maya Chen, VP of Operations
**Data:** Jan–Mar 2024 NYC TLC yellow + green taxi trip data
**Goal:** Deploy 50 drivers to maximize revenue and market entry success

---

## Executive Summary

**Deploy 50 drivers across 5 zones, split into two shifts, with 5–8 drivers at airports as a premium anchor.**

Based on 9M+ taxi trips analyzed from Q1 2024, SwiftRide should:

1. **Target JFK Airport (8 drivers)** — $81 avg fare, 137K monthly trips, green cabs barely present
2. **Deploy to Midtown Center (12 drivers)** — highest volume zone, 135K trips/month
3. **Cover Times Square/Theatre District (10 drivers)** — strong weekend hold, $27 avg fare
4. **Position at Upper East Side South (10 drivers)** — 135K trips, residential consistency
5. **Anchor LaGuardia Airport (5 drivers)** — $66 avg fare, complement JFK coverage

**Remaining 5 drivers** should cover the fastest-growing zones: Downtown Brooklyn (+45% growth), Alphabet City (+44%), and Seaport (+33%).

### Hourly Deployment Plan

**Weekday Shifts:**
- **Morning (7–10 AM):** 15 drivers — commuter rush, 90K–130K trips/hour
- **Evening (4–8 PM):** 20 drivers — peak demand, 178K–196K trips/hour, $28–$30 avg

**Weekend Shifts:**
- **Afternoon (2–6 PM):** 10 drivers — entertainment peak, 43K–46K trips/hour
- **Late Night (10 PM–2 AM):** 5 drivers —酒吧/restaurant traffic, $25–$29 avg

### Trip Profile Strategy

| Distance | Trip Share | Revenue Share | Avg Fare | Recommendation |
|----------|------------|---------------|----------|----------------|
| 0–1 mi | 24% | 12% | $14 | Volume play |
| 1–2 mi | 34% | 23% | $19 | **Sweet spot** |
| 2–3 mi | 16% | 14% | $24 | **Sweet spot** |
| 3–5 mi | 11% | 12% | $31 | Good margin |
| 10–20 mi | 7% | 21% | $82 | Airport runs |

**Position for 1–3 mile urban hops** — 74% of trips, efficient revenue per mile ($10–$13/mi vs $5/mi for long haul).

### Competitive Positioning

- **Yellow cabs own Manhattan core:** 100K+ trips/zone in Midtown, Upper East Side
- **Green cabs cluster in gaps:** East Harlem (12K), Queens (3K), Brooklyn (2K)
- **SwiftRide strategy:** Avoid direct yellow competition in core, target airport premium or growing zones

### The April Bet

- **March momentum:** +11.6% trips, +15.5% revenue vs February
- **Fastest-growing zones:** Downtown Brooklyn (+45%), Alphabet City (+44%), Seaport (+33%)
- **Launch timing:** Market is expanding — April is the right window

## Risk Register

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Weather/events disrupt April demand | Medium | Jan–Mar data doesn't capture spring weather; monitor weekly |
| Yellow cabs retaliate with surge pricing | Medium | Focus on airport premium (regulated fares) and growing zones |
| Green cabs expand into target zones | Low | Green volume is 50x lower than yellow — limited threat |
| Driver retention below 50 | High | Offer airport shifts (higher tips) as retention tool |
| Airport authorization delays | Medium | Start with city zones, add airports in Month 2 |
| Zone demand shifts seasonally | Medium | Re-run analysis quarterly; summer may favor different zones |

## Plan B: Alternative Deployment

If the primary bet fails (airport authorization denied, Midtown too competitive):

**Alternative Zone Set:**
1. **East Harlem North** — green cab territory, 12K trips/month, $20 avg
2. **Forest Hills** — Queens residential, 2.8K green trips, $20 avg
3. **Downtown Brooklyn/MetroTech** — fastest growing (+45%), $24 avg
4. **Alphabet City** — growing (+44%), nightlife demand
5. **Central Park** — tourist traffic, 2.7K green trips, $22 avg

**Plan B strategy:** Target the "green gap" — zones where green cabs operate but yellow doesn't dominate. Lower volume but less competition.

## Assumptions and Caveats

- **Data period:** Jan–Mar 2024 only — April weather/Easter not captured
- **Fare structure:** Assumes SwiftRide pricing matches TLC benchmarks
- **Driver supply:** Assumes 50 drivers can be recruited and retained
- **Green taxi data:** Only 56K trips/month — insights have higher uncertainty
- **Zone sentinels:** "N/A" and "Unknown" zones excluded from analysis
- **Outliers filtered:** Trips with fare ≤ 0, distance > 100mi, or passenger_count null/0 excluded
- **Revenue estimates:** Based on average fares — actual revenue varies by time, demand, and driver performance
