# Zone Strategy — Which neighborhoods/zones should SwiftRide prioritize first, and which should they avoid?

> Question 1 of 7 from the analysis intention for **swiftride-nyc-playbook**.

**Bottom line:** SwiftRide should deploy drivers to 5 high-opportunity zones that balance volume, revenue, and competitive gaps. JFK Airport is the clear #1 — high fares, predictable demand, and green taxis barely operate there. Midtown Manhattan offers volume but faces intense yellow cab competition.

---

## Zone Volume Ranking (Jan 2024)

Which zones have the most taxi activity?

```sql q1_zone_volume
SELECT 
  z.Borough,
  z.Zone,
  z.service_zone,
  COUNT(*) as trip_count,
  ROUND(AVG(c.total_amount), 2) as avg_total,
  ROUND(AVG(c.tip_amount), 2) as avg_tip,
  ROUND(AVG(c.trip_distance), 2) as avg_distance,
  ROUND(SUM(c.total_amount), 0) as total_revenue
FROM (
  SELECT * FROM files.yellow_tripdata_2024_01
  WHERE fare_amount > 0 AND trip_distance > 0 AND trip_distance < 100
    AND passenger_count > 0
) c
JOIN files.taxi_zone_lookup z ON c.PULocationID = z.LocationID
GROUP BY z.Borough, z.Zone, z.service_zone
ORDER BY trip_count DESC
LIMIT 15
```

<DataTable data={q1_zone_volume} rows=15/>

**Key insight:** JFK Airport leads with 137K trips and $81 avg total — 4x the average Manhattan zone fare. But Manhattan zones dominate the top 15 by volume.

## Zone Opportunity Score

Raw volume doesn't tell the full story. Which zones offer the best combination of demand AND revenue per trip?

```sql q1_opportunity_score
SELECT 
  z.Borough,
  z.Zone,
  z.service_zone,
  COUNT(*) as trip_count,
  ROUND(AVG(c.total_amount), 2) as avg_total,
  ROUND(SUM(c.total_amount), 0) as total_revenue,
  ROUND(COUNT(*) * AVG(c.total_amount), 0) as opportunity_score
FROM (
  SELECT * FROM files.yellow_tripdata_2024_01
  WHERE fare_amount > 0 AND trip_distance > 0 AND trip_distance < 100
    AND passenger_count > 0
) c
JOIN files.taxi_zone_lookup z ON c.PULocationID = z.LocationID
GROUP BY z.Borough, z.Zone, z.service_zone
HAVING trip_count > 1000
ORDER BY opportunity_score DESC
LIMIT 15
```

<BarChart data={q1_opportunity_score} x=Zone y=opportunity_score series=Borough title="Top 15 Zones by Opportunity Score (Trips × Avg Fare)" />

**What this means:** JFK Airport has the highest opportunity score ($11M) due to premium fares. Midtown Center and Upper East Side follow on pure volume.

## Borough Breakdown

Which boroughs should SwiftRide focus on?

```sql q1_borough_summary
SELECT 
  z.Borough,
  COUNT(*) as trip_count,
  ROUND(AVG(c.total_amount), 2) as avg_total,
  ROUND(SUM(c.total_amount), 0) as total_revenue,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(*) / SUM(COUNT(*)) OVER(), 1) as trip_share_pct
FROM (
  SELECT * FROM files.yellow_tripdata_2024_01
  WHERE fare_amount > 0 AND trip_distance > 0 AND trip_distance < 100
    AND passenger_count > 0
) c
JOIN files.taxi_zone_lookup z ON c.PULocationID = z.LocationID
WHERE z.Borough NOT IN ('N/A', 'Unknown')
GROUP BY z.Borough
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
```

<BarChart data={q1_borough_summary} x=Borough y=total_revenue title="Total Revenue by Borough (Jan 2024)" />

**Manhattan dominates** with 87% of all trips and $65M in revenue. Queens is second at 8%, boosted by airport traffic. Brooklyn, Bronx, and Staten Island are marginal.

## Competitive Gap Zones (Yellow vs Green)

Where are green taxis active? These are zones where yellow cabs are less dominant — potential opportunity for SwiftRide.

```sql q1_green_zones
SELECT 
  z.Borough,
  z.Zone,
  COUNT(*) as green_trips,
  ROUND(AVG(c.total_amount), 2) as avg_total
FROM (
  SELECT PULocationID, total_amount FROM files.green_tripdata_2024_01
  WHERE fare_amount > 0 AND trip_distance > 0 AND trip_distance < 100
) c
JOIN files.taxi_zone_lookup z ON c.PULocationID = z.LocationID
GROUP BY z.Borough, z.Zone
ORDER BY green_trips DESC
LIMIT 10
```

<BarChart data={q1_green_zones} x=Zone y=green_trips series=Borough title="Top Green Taxi Zones — Where Yellow Cabs Are Weaker" />

**Key finding:** Green taxis cluster in East Harlem, Queens, and Brooklyn — areas where yellow cabs have less presence. These are "gap zones" where SwiftRide could avoid direct yellow competition.

---

## Findings

- **JFK Airport is the #1 deployment target:** $81 avg fare, 137K monthly trips, predictable demand, green taxis barely operate there
- **Midtown Manhattan offers volume but fierce competition:** 100K+ trips per zone, but yellow cabs completely dominate
- **Green taxi gap zones exist:** East Harlem, Forest Hills, Fort Greene — underserved by yellows, active for greens
- **Manhattan = 87% of market:** Outer boroughs are marginal except Queens (airport-driven)
- **Opportunity = volume × revenue × competitive gap:** Pure volume ranking misses JFK's premium fares

---

## Notes

- Data filtered: fare_amount > 0, trip_distance 0–100mi, passenger_count > 0
- "N/A" and "Unknown" boroughs excluded
- Green taxi data (56K trips/month) has higher uncertainty than yellow (3M trips/month)
- Zone opportunity score is simplified — real deployment would factor in driver supply, surge patterns, and time-of-day
